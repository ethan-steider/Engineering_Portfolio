Capstone: Modular UAS Platform for Environmental Research

Capstone • Aug 2025 – May 2026

Designed and tested a modular unmanned aerial system for environmental data collection. The platform integrates flight control, onboard compute, sensing, and communications to support field deployments and iterative system testing.

What I Worked On

  • Integrated flight computer, onboard compute, sensors, and communications into a single UAS platform.
  • Debugged GPS, telemetry, and power issues affecting autonomous flight and system reliability.
  • Developed and iterated a browser-based ground station UI for live video and sensor monitoring.
  • Planned and executed ground and flight tests to validate system performance.

System Architecture

  • Flight Control: Pixhawk Cube Orange+ running ArduPilot.
  • Compute: NVIDIA Jetson Nano (onboard processing and networking).
  • Sensing: Optical camera, thermal camera, and environmental sensors.
  • Communications: 2.4 GHz LAN using directional antennas for data and video.

Testing & Current Status

  • Completed initial flight tests validating hover stability, GPS lock, and telemetry.
  • Ground station UI implemented with multiple iterations (V1–V3).
  • Antenna characterization, link budgets, and range testing currently in progress.
